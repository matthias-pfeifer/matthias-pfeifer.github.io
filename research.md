<section id="research">
    <h2>Research Interests</h2>
    <p>
        I am broadly interested in algebraic geometry. My current focus is on deformations of singularities, particularly in positive and mixed characteristic. As doctoral project, I was able to prove that the class of <span title="which in dimension 2 are exactly the toric surface singularities">isolated cyclic (lrq) singularities</span> is stable under deformations in positive and mixed characteristic, generalizing the result in char 0.
    </p>
    <p style="margin-top: 10px">
        My doctoral project sparked a number of follow-up questions:
    <ul style="margin-left: 20px; margin-top: 10px;">
        <li>What can we say about deformations of singularities arising as quotients of non-cyclic groups? If a deformation of a quotient singularity is again a quotient singularity, what can we say about the associated groups?</li>
        <li style="margin-top: 6px">What about deformations of (isolated) toric singularities in higher dimension?</li>
        <li style="margin-top: 6px">What is the relation between versal deformations and deformations over a complete DVR?</li>
    </ul>
    </p>
